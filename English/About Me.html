About Me

My name is César Romeo, and I am currently a student at Niederrhein University in Krefeld, Germany, where I am taking the orientation semester with the goal of starting a degree in Computer Engineering in October.

I was born in Zaragoza, Spain, the city where I lived for most of my life. My interest in computing began at the age of 15, driven by curiosity and the enthusiasm to create my own video games. At that time, I discovered the Roblox platform, where I took my first steps in programming using the Lua language. I developed my own clicker game with a simple interface and basic functionalities, which allowed me to familiarize myself with programming logic and interface development.

Later, I participated in a robotics course where, using LEGO pieces and sensors with motors, we designed an explorer robot. This project introduced me to the concepts of automation and programming applied in a physical environment, further strengthening my interest in technology.

During my school years, I also used tools such as Scratch and App Inventor, which allowed me to experiment with creating mobile applications and games, solidifying my self-learning abilities.

With the arrival of the pandemic, I moved to Germany, where I focused my efforts on learning the language and adapting to the environment. During this period, I worked in various sectors and took German classes in both academies and work environments. One of the most enriching experiences was volunteering at a youth center, where I was able to significantly improve my communication skills in German.

In recent months, I have rekindled my passion for computing and have decided to focus my professional career in this field. I am taking advantage of this orientation semester to reinforce my knowledge and prepare for the start of my degree, with the intention of combining my studies with a job opportunity that will allow me to gain practical experience in the sector.
